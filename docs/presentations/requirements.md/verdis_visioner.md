## Format

- 2-minute pitch on stage
- Followed by Q&A dialogue with a judge panel
- Audience votes via mobile phone; winner is crowned live
- Optional tech brief at 10:30–11:00 (test scene, monitor, slides, flow)

## Judging Criteria

The audience and judges evaluate on:

1. **Innovationshøjde** — How novel and differentiating is the concept?
2. **Markedspotentiale** — How large is the potential market, and who pays?
3. **Markedsmodenhed (timing)** — How ready is the solution, and why is the timing right now?
4. **Impact** — To what degree does the idea make a concrete difference for sustainability?

## Pitch Structure Guidelines (2 minutes)

The organizer recommends the following structure:

1. **Start sharp:** problem, solution, and what makes Easysort unique
2. **Be concrete on the customer:** who pays, and why?
3. **Show why now:** what makes the timing right at this moment?
4. **Back it up with 1–2 data points:** pilot, customer, results, or other momentum proof
5. **Impact without vague headlines:** make it measurable (what is reduced/improved, how much, at what scale?)
6. **End clearly:** what is the desired next step after the event?

## Presentation Requirements

- **Duration:** Strictly 2 minutes — every second counts
- **Format:** Slidev presentation, exportable to 16:9 PDF
- **Tone:** Confident, concrete, data-driven. Avoid generic sustainability claims.
- **Language:** Danish (audience and judges are Danish-speaking)

### Content the pitch must cover

1. **Problem:** Denmark burns 3.1 million tons of waste per year. Household recycling rate is only 55%. EU pushed sorting onto citizens instead of funding infrastructure. The waste industry is far behind on AI.
2. **Solution:** Easysort builds one open-source foundation model for waste recognition. From that base, specific models are created for any application — recycling centers, transfer stations, waste guides.
3. **What makes us unique:** Only company building open-source AI for waste. Models, software and integrations are freely available. All servers in EU/Copenhagen. Anyone can self-host.
4. **Who pays:** Municipalities and waste facilities pay for implementation and service. They can always go independent with the open-source tools.
5. **Traction (use in pitch):** 1.8M images analyzed, 1.1M objects detected/sorted, 34 live devices. Name customers: Verdis, Argo, KK, Norfors, Econet, Arc, DTU. Do NOT state exact revenue figures on stage — save for judge Q&A.
6. **Why now:** AI vision models are finally good enough. The waste industry needs this push. Timing is right.
7. **Impact (measurable):** Reducing residual waste and impurities at facilities, enabling data-driven optimization of waste streams, making the technology freely available to everyone.
8. **The ask (closing):** Contributors and developers to help build, new customers and pilot projects, people with ideas for new applications, partnerships.
